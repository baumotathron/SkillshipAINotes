üÜö Product vs Service

Core Distinctions

  • Product: Standardised offering with consistent pricing and usage. Low variable cost, automation-driven, tangible (physical or digital). Examples: Swiggy app, iPhone, ChatGPT.
  • Service: Custom, human-driven, higher variable cost. Personalised experiences with higher ticket sizes. Examples: consulting, delivery, teaching.

Core Idea

  • Product ‚Üí Scalable, repeatable, automated value.
  • Service ‚Üí Personalised, expertise-based human value.

AI increasingly blurs this line. Personalised Spotify playlists or Canva‚Äôs auto-generated designs make products feel like services, while traditionally human-led services adopt automation to scale thoughtfully.

üöÄ What is Product Management?

The Practice

Product Management is the art and science of solving meaningful problems at the intersection of business, design, and technology ‚Äî think of it as managing a digital dhandha.

A Product Manager constantly answers three core questions:

  1. Who is our user?
  2. What problem are we solving?
  3. How will we know it worked?

Seven Critical Functions

  1. Define the problem
  2. Research the market and users
  3. Frame compelling solutions
  4. Design the product experience (UI/UX)
  5. Partner with engineering to build
  6. Collaborate on marketing & go-to-market
  7. Measure outcomes and iterate

Swiggy: Grounding in Reality

  • Problem: Ordering food easily.
  • Solution: One app that consolidates menus, payments, and real-time delivery tracking.
  • PM‚Äôs Contribution: Aligns design, engineering, marketing, and strategy on the same user promise.

Case Study 1 ‚Äì Zomato

üß© Identifying Problems for Product Ideas

‚ÄúBefore a great product is built, a great problem is understood.‚Äù

Mindsets of a Problem Identifier

  • Start from pain, not product: Ask why people are frustrated before sketching solutions.
  • Spot inefficiencies and workarounds: Every complaint hides an opportunity.
  • Track patterns: Repeated friction across contexts is a strong signal.
  • Zoom out: Validate that the problem scales beyond a personal inconvenience.
