üìù Getting Started

üéØ From Problem Statement to Market Validation

You've identified a problem using this format: "[User/segment] is struggling with [problem] because [cause], which leads to [impact]"

Now what? These templates help you move from problem identification to deep market understanding. Each prompt is designed to uncover specific insights that will either validate your problem or help you pivot to something more promising.

Remember: The goal isn't to prove you're right - it's to discover the truth about your market and users.

‚úÖ Problem Validation Prompts

Template 1: Root Cause Analysis (5 Whys)

Ensure you're solving the root cause, not just symptoms.

Problem Statement: [YOUR PROBLEM STATEMENT] Conduct a thorough root cause analysis: 1. Apply the 5 Whys methodology to dig deeper 2. Identify if this is a symptom or root cause 3. Map out the cause-and-effect chain 4. Determine if solving this addresses the fundamental issue 5. Suggest data points or user testimonials needed to validate root cause 6. Provide credible sources and research links to support your analysis Provide multiple "why" chains if there could be different root causes. Include URLs to relevant research papers, articles, or data sources.
Copied!

Template 2: Problem-Solution Fit Assessment

Get an honest evaluation of whether your problem is worth solving.

Problem Statement: [YOUR PROBLEM STATEMENT] Research Data Summary: [PASTE YOUR KEY RESEARCH FINDINGS] Evaluate problem-solution fit: 1. Is this a problem worth solving? (Severity, frequency, willingness to pay) 2. Is the market large enough? (TAM, SAM, SOM) 3. Is the timing right? (Market trends, technology readiness) 4. Are there alternative solutions that work well enough? 5. What evidence supports that users would adopt a new solution? 6. What are the biggest risks to problem-solution fit? 7. On a scale of 1-10, how confident should I be in pursuing this? 8. What additional validation is needed? 9. Provide URLs to market reports, studies, or research that support your assessment Provide honest, critical assessment with credible sources and links.
Copied!

üìä Secondary Research Prompts

Template 3: Data & Evidence Discovery

Find data, statistics, and research that supports or challenges your problem.

Problem Statement: [YOUR PROBLEM STATEMENT] Help me find credible data and evidence about this problem: 1. Find statistics, reports, and research studies that prove this problem exists 2. What is the scale/magnitude of this problem? (number of affected users, financial impact, time lost, etc.) 3. Identify trends over time (is this problem getting worse, better, or staying the same?) 4. Find user testimonials, forum discussions, or social media conversations where people mention this problem 5. Locate industry reports, market research, or academic papers related to this problem 6. Provide URLs/links to all sources for verification For each data point, include: - The specific statistic or finding - Source name and credibility - Direct URL link to the source - Publication date/recency Focus on recent data (2020-2025) and credible sources.
Copied!

Template 4: Market Size & Opportunity Analysis

Understand the total market potential with data sources.

Problem Statement: [YOUR PROBLEM STATEMENT] Please help me understand the market opportunity: 1. What is the total addressable market (TAM) for this problem? 2. What is the serviceable addressable market (SAM) - who can I realistically reach? 3. What is the serviceable obtainable market (SOM) - what market share is achievable in year 1? 4. What are the market trends related to this problem in India/globally? 5. Provide specific data sources, statistics, and market research reports 6. Include URLs to industry reports, market research firms, or government data Please structure your response with clear numbers, sources (with URLs), and realistic assumptions based on credible data.
Copied!

Template 5: Competitive Landscape Analysis

Identify competitors with source links.

Problem Statement: [YOUR PROBLEM STATEMENT] Help me analyze the competitive landscape: 1. Who are the direct competitors solving this problem? (Include company websites) 2. Who are the indirect competitors (alternative solutions)? 3. What are their key features, pricing, and target users? 4. What are their strengths and weaknesses? 5. What gaps exist in current solutions that I can exploit? 6. Create a competitive positioning matrix 7. Provide URLs to competitor websites, product pages, and recent reviews/articles about them 8. Include links to competitor analysis reports or market research Focus on Indian market context where relevant, and provide credible source links for all competitor information.
Copied!

Template 6: Industry & Trend Analysis

Understand trends with source documentation.

Problem Statement: [YOUR PROBLEM STATEMENT] Research and provide: 1. Current industry trends (2024-2025) related to this problem 2. Regulatory and legal considerations in India 3. Technological advancements enabling solutions 4. Social and behavioral shifts affecting this problem 5. Economic factors influencing the market 6. Expert opinions and thought leadership articles 7. For each point, provide URLs to credible sources: industry reports, news articles, research papers, expert blogs Include recent data with proper source attribution and links for verification.
Copied!

üë• User Research Prompts

Template 7: User Persona Development

Create detailed, research-backed user personas.

Problem Statement: [YOUR PROBLEM STATEMENT] Help me create detailed user personas: 1. Identify 3 different user segments facing this problem 2. For each segment, provide: - Demographics (age, location, income, education) - Psychographics (values, motivations, lifestyle) - Behavioral patterns (how they currently solve this problem) - Pain points and frustrations - Goals and desired outcomes - Technology adoption level - Buying triggers and decision factors 3. Include references to research studies or demographic data that support these persona characteristics 4. Provide URLs to relevant market research or demographic reports Make personas realistic and India-specific where applicable, backed by actual market data.
Copied!

Template 8: User Behavior & Motivation Analysis

Understand what drives your users and their current behaviors.

Problem Statement: [YOUR PROBLEM STATEMENT] Help me understand user behavior and motivation: 1. What are the key behaviors users exhibit related to this problem? 2. What motivates users to seek a solution? (functional, emotional, social drivers) 3. What triggers users to look for solutions right now? 4. What are the barriers preventing users from solving this problem? 5. How do users currently cope with or work around this problem? 6. What are the "jobs" users are trying to get done? (functional, emotional, social) 7. What would make users switch from their current solution to a new one? 8. Provide links to behavioral research, psychology studies, or user behavior reports relevant to this problem Include credible sources and research URLs to support behavioral insights.
Copied!

üîç Primary Research: Design, Conduct & Analyze

Template 9: User Interview Guide Design

Design comprehensive user interview questions following best practices.

Problem Statement: [YOUR PROBLEM STATEMENT] Create a comprehensive user interview guide: 1. Opening questions (5) - Build rapport and context 2. Experience questions (8-10) - Understand current behavior and past experiences 3. Pain point questions (5-7) - Uncover frustrations and challenges 4. Motivation questions (5) - Understand why they behave the way they do 5. Solution exploration (3-5) - Test receptiveness without leading 6. Closing questions (2-3) - Open-ended insights For each question, provide: - The exact question to ask - What you're trying to learn - Follow-up probes if needed - Common pitfalls to avoid Ensure questions are open-ended and unbiased (following The Mom Test principles). Include best practices from user research methodologies.
Copied!

Template 10: Conducting User Interviews - Best Practices

Learn how to conduct effective user interviews.

Problem Statement: [YOUR PROBLEM STATEMENT] I'm preparing to conduct user interviews. Provide guidance on: **Before the Interview:** 1. How to recruit the right participants? 2. What to prepare (materials, tools, scripts)? 3. How to schedule and set expectations? **During the Interview:** 1. How to build rapport and make users comfortable? 2. How to ask unbiased questions that reveal truth? 3. What body language and tone to use? 4. How to handle difficult or shy participants? 5. How to dig deeper without leading? 6. What red flags indicate biased responses? **Interview Best Practices:** 1. The Mom Test principles - avoid leading questions 2. How to listen more than you talk 3. When to ask "why" and when not to 4. How to document insights during interviews Provide practical, actionable advice with examples.
Copied!

Template 11: Analyzing Interview Results

Extract patterns and actionable insights from your interview data.

Problem Statement: [YOUR PROBLEM STATEMENT] I conducted [NUMBER] user interviews. Here are my notes: [PASTE YOUR INTERVIEW NOTES/QUOTES] Please help me analyze these interviews: **Pattern Recognition:** 1. What are the common themes across interviews? 2. What pain points are mentioned most frequently? 3. What surprising or unexpected insights emerged? 4. Are there contradictions or conflicting viewpoints? **Insight Extraction:** 1. What are the stated needs (what users say they want)? 2. What are the unstated needs (what users actually need)? 3. What emotional drivers are present? 4. What workarounds or hacks are users currently using? **Segmentation:** 1. Can you identify different user segments based on responses? 2. What characterizes each segment? 3. Which segment(s) feel the pain most acutely? **Validation Assessment:** 1. Does this data validate my problem statement? 2. What should I change or pivot based on these insights? 3. What additional questions do I need to ask? 4. How many more interviews do I need? **Actionable Next Steps:** Provide specific recommendations on what to do with these insights. Structure your analysis with direct quotes supporting each theme.
Copied!

üí° Best Practices & Pro Tips

üöÄ How to Get the Most from These Templates

  • Start with validation: Use Problem Validation prompts first to ensure you're solving a real problem
  • Gather evidence: Use Secondary Research to find data that supports or challenges your assumptions
  • Understand users deeply: Use User Research prompts to build empathy and understanding
  • Go talk to real users: Use Primary Research guides to conduct interviews and validate with real people
  • Always ask for sources: Request URLs and links to verify AI-provided information
  • Iterate and refine: Use AI responses to create follow-up questions and deeper prompts
  • Cross-validate insights: Use multiple templates to verify important findings
  • Question the AI: Challenge assumptions and request clarification
  • Document everything: Keep all responses organized for easy reference

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Reminders

  • AI is a research assistant, not an oracle: Always validate AI insights with real data and user conversations
  • Replace ALL placeholders: Don't leave any [BRACKETS] in your final prompts
  • Verify sources: Check that provided URLs are real and credible
  • Stay objective: Seek honest, critical feedback - don't look for validation
  • Primary research is essential: No amount of secondary research replaces talking to real users
